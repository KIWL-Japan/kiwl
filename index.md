<section class="history-story">
  <div class="section-heading">
    <p class="eyebrow">Origin</p>
    <h2>How KIWL started</h2>
  </div>
  <div class="story-card">
    <p>The Knights in White Lycra was formed in 2013 by a few British men needing to get fit and wanting to give back. A karaoke rendering of the 1960s hit "Nights in White Satin" inspired the name for what they assumed would be a one-off bicycle ride.</p>
    <p>That first ride became the beginning of a much bigger volunteer effort built around the idea of getting fit and giving back.</p>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="history-timeline">
  <div class="section-heading">
    <p class="eyebrow">Milestones</p>
    <h2>Major rides and fundraising history</h2>
  </div>
  <div class="timeline-list">
    <article class="timeline-card">
      <p class="timeline-year">2013</p>
      <p>10 riders cycled 330km to Minamisoma in Tohoku and raised ¥2.7 million for the Save Minamisoma Project, supporting people living in temporary accommodation after the disaster.</p>
    </article>

    <article class="timeline-card">
      <p class="timeline-year">2014</p>
      <p>20 cyclists rode 500km from Tokyo to Minamisanriku and raised ¥5.5 million for OGA for Aid to build a vegetable processing unit.</p>
    </article>

    <article class="timeline-card">
      <p class="timeline-year">2015</p>
      <p>The peloton grew to 26 cyclists and completed the same journey for Place to Grow, raising ¥7.4 million to support agricultural education for local children.</p>
    </article>

    <article class="timeline-card">
      <p class="timeline-year">2016 to 2018</p>
      <p>KIWL partnered with Mirai no Mori in 2016, raising ¥10 million on a ride from Tokyo to an Iwate care home. The 2017 ride raised ¥14.4 million, and the 2018 KIWL 500 from Nikko to Ichinoseki raised ¥12 million for Mirai no Mori's Leader in Training program while also helping bring Frame Football to Japan with NPO Esperanza.</p>
    </article>

    <article class="timeline-card">
      <p class="timeline-year">2019</p>
      <p>42 riders completed a new and demanding route. Together with fringe events, the year raised a record ¥14.5 million for YouMeWe NPO.</p>
    </article>

    <article class="timeline-card">
      <p class="timeline-year">2020 to 2021</p>
      <p>Even with many in-person events cancelled during the global pandemic, generous sponsors, virtual events, solo efforts, and Global Giving matching helped KIWL raise ¥30.7 million across the two years.</p>
    </article>

    <article class="timeline-card">
      <p class="timeline-year">2022</p>
      <p>Full operations resumed with 38 riders taking on a route from Tokyo to Kanazawa via Karuizawa, Hakuba, and Toyama.</p>
    </article>

    <article class="timeline-card">
      <p class="timeline-year">2023</p>
      <p>The 10th anniversary ride brought together 47 cycling volunteers from 14 countries, including the British Ambassador to Japan, on a route from Utsunomiya through Koriyama, Fukushima, and Sendai to a children's home in Ichinoseki.</p>
    </article>

    <article class="timeline-card">
      <p class="timeline-year">2024</p>
      <p>The route from Shizuoka to Nagano City via Toyokawa, Ena, and Lake Suwa drew 45 cyclists and 5 support crew through the countryside and the historic Nakasendo road. It became KIWL's most successful year yet, raising ¥20 million.</p>
    </article>

    <article class="timeline-card">
      <p class="timeline-year">2025</p>
      <p>The latest ride, completed on June 1, 2025, took the Knights of Noto from Kanazawa on a 400km-plus loop via Wajima and Nanao. Alongside the ride, KIWL and the BCCJ visited Wajima to support residents affected by the 2024 earthquake, donating international snacks for children and spending time with games and activities.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="history-summary">
  <div class="summary-card">
    <p class="eyebrow">Today</p>
    <h2>Continuing the mission</h2>
    <p>Through the annual cycle ride and many other sporting and social events, KIWL has raised over ¥187,460,000 for disadvantaged children in Japan as of December 31, 2025.</p>
    <p>KIWL welcomes volunteers of any age and ability who want to get fit and give back while helping empower fragile children to become successful and confident young adults.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="summary-card">
    <p class="eyebrow">General Information</p>
    <h2>How KIWL operates</h2>
    <ul class="info-list">
      <li>The Knights in White Lycra is a collective name for volunteers who raise funds by participating in events organized with its partnering charity.</li>
      <li>Volunteers join events willingly and entirely at their own risk.</li>
      <li>KIWL does not accept donations directly and only pools money from participants to cover event costs.</li>
      <li>KIWL is not a legal entity, has no employees, is not profit-making, and is not a registered charity in its own right.</li>
      <li>KIWL is not a licensed tour operator. Events are organized by KIWL volunteers for participants.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</section>
